    Section2: What Makes or Breaks a Trip: Visitor Experience Analysis

    Every destination wants happy visitors, but few know what actually creates satisfaction. We analyzed thousands of New Zealand traveler reviews to indicate the exact factors that turn good trips into unforgettable ones

    This experience dashboard leverages NLP and sentiment analysis techniques to process thousands of visitor feedback comments. We applied text processing, sentiment analysis, and topic modeling to transform unstructured text data into actionable insights.

    NLP & Sentiment Analysis Methodology:

    • Text Processing Pipeline: Cleaned and preprocessed visitor survey text data (tokenization, stopword removal, lemmatization)
    • Sentiment Analysis: Applied VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary for sEntiment Reasoning) lexicon-based algorithm to analyze emotional tone of comments, generating compound sentiment scores between -1 (extremely negative) and +1 (extremely positive)
    • Topic Modeling: Implemented Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), an unsupervised machine learning technique, to discover abstract topics within the feedback text and cluster similar comments into coherent thematic categories
    • Impact Classification: Developed custom algorithm to transform raw sentiment scores into four impact levels:
      • Low Impact: Sentiment scores between -0.2 and +0.2, indicating minimal effect on overall experience
      • Moderate Impact: Sentiment scores between -0.5 to -0.2 or +0.2 to +0.5, showing notable influence
      • High Impact: Sentiment scores between -0.8 to -0.5 or +0.5 to +0.8, representing significant effect
      • Critical Impact: Sentiment scores below -0.8 or above +0.8, indicating experience-defining factors
    • Statistical Validation: Applied chi-square tests to verify significant correlations between identified factors and overall satisfaction ratings (p < 0.01)

    Key Findings:

    Nature activities and safety create the best travel memories (94% and 92% satisfaction), while poor amenities and safety concerns ruin more trips than anything else. The data shows a clear pattern: travelers value authentic experiences and feeling secure above luxury amenities.

    Visualization Guide:

    • Green bar represent positive travel experiences (higher % = more satisfied visitors)
    • Red bar represent negative travel experiences (lower % = major problem areas)
    • Circle size represents word frequency - larger circles indicate words mentioned more often by tourists
    • Impact levels show trip impact based on sentiment analysis of visitor text feedback
    • Actionable Insights: For Travelers: Prioritize destinations with strong safety records and nature access. Budget less for fancy amenities, more for quality activities. For Destinations: Invest in safety infrastructure and nature experience quality: these drive satisfaction more than expensive facilities. Safety is make-or-break: 92% satisfaction when good, only 27% when bad: the biggest swing of any factor.
